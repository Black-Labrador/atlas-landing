Without hedging

A lithium producer in Chile needs $100M to expand. Western banks say: "How do we know you can service the debt if lithium drops 60%?" Without a hedge, the bank says no — or demands 15%+ interest. The producer's only option is a Chinese offtaker who'll finance in exchange for 80% of production at a steep discount. Locked in. Single buyer dependency.

With Atlas

The same producer hedges 60% of output at $11,000/ton on Atlas. Goes back to the bank: "Revenue is locked in. Here's the position." Bank lends at 9%, lighter covenants, bigger facility. The producer saves $6M/year in financing costs and keeps their offtake freedom. No Chinese dependency.

"Policy secures supply. Atlas makes it tradable."

The US government just backed a $1.8B consortium (Orion CMC) to acquire critical mineral assets globally — including a $9B stake in Glencore's DRC cobalt operations, announced February 2026. The IRA, EU CRMA, and CHIPS Act are channeling hundreds of billions toward supply diversification. But capital without market infrastructure is capital without leverage. Atlas builds the missing financial layer.
